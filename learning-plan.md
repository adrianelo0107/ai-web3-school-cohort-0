# Personal Learning Plan

本计划基于 Handbook 的四层结构：AI 基础、Web3 基础、AI x Web3 Bridge、前沿探索。因为我的 AI 和 Web3 都已有基础，计划会压缩复习时间，把主要精力放在产品研究、AI x Web3 应用模式和 Hackathon 可交付成果上。

This plan follows the Handbook layers: AI foundations, Web3 foundations, AI x Web3 bridge, and frontier exploration. Since I already have intermediate AI and Web3 knowledge, the plan compresses review time and emphasizes product research, application patterns, and hackathon deliverables.

## Daily 2-Hour Loop

| Block | Time | Output |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Review and plan | 10 min | Update today's task list |
| Handbook reading | 35 min | 3-5 notes, 1 question |
| Product research | 30 min | Problem, user, market, or competitor note |
| Build or experiment | 35 min | Script, prototype, prompt, or workflow artifact |
| Check-in and feedback | 10 min | Daily draft + feedback candidate |

## 8-Week Plan

| Week | Theme | Focus | Deliverable |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Setup | Learning repo, profile, daily rhythm, feedback workflow | Public repo + first check-in draft |
| 1 | AI product thinking | Agents, workflows, evaluation, user problems | 3 product opportunity notes |
| 2 | Web3 refresher | Wallets, on-chain data, smart contract UX, security assumptions | Web3 concept map + risk checklist |
| 3 | AI x Web3 bridge | Agent-wallet interactions, on-chain data analysis, decentralized identity ideas | 2 small experiments |
| 4 | Hackathon problem selection | User pain, wedge, scope, feasibility | 1-page product brief |
| 5 | Prototype sprint | Minimal demo, scripted user journey, evaluation criteria | Demo prototype or clickable workflow |
| 6 | Research and narrative | Market map, comparable projects, value proposition | Pitch outline + research memo |
| 7 | Submission polish | Demo video script, README, roadmap, feedback incorporation | Hackathon submission pack |

## Weekly Review Questions

- What did I learn that changed my product assumptions?
- What did I build or test, even if small?
- What Handbook section created confusion or deserved improvement?
- What would make this project understandable to another builder in 5 minutes?
- What is the smallest next experiment that reduces uncertainty?

## Success Criteria

- At least 5 daily check-in drafts per week.
- At least 1 Handbook feedback candidate per week.
- At least 1 build or research artifact per week.
- One scoped hackathon idea by the end of Week 4.
- One demo-ready prototype or narrative by the end of Week 7.
